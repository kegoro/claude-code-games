Writing Practice

Task 1 (20 min · 150 words min)  ·  Task 2 (40 min · 250 words min)

Task 1 — Academic

Timer: 20:00
0 / 150 words minimum

Task 1 Band Descriptors

5
Task Achievement: Covers main features but may be inaccurate or unclear. Limited overview.
6
Task Achievement: Addresses task adequately. Overview present but not fully developed. Key features covered.
7
Task Achievement: Clear overview. Main trends identified. Minor inaccuracies. Data clearly selected.
8
Task Achievement: Fully addresses all requirements. Well-developed overview. Relevant data accurately selected and highlighted.

Task 1 Formula

  • Para 1 — Paraphrase the question in your own words (1–2 sentences)
  • Para 2 — Overview of the most striking trends/features (no data, just the big picture)
  • Para 3 — Detail 1 with specific figures, comparisons, and trends
  • Para 4 — Detail 2 with remaining key data points
  • Use precise language: rose sharply, declined gradually, remained relatively stable, peaked at, accounted for

Task 2 — Essay

Timer: 40:00
0 / 250 words minimum

Task 2 Band Descriptors

5
Position is unclear. Ideas are limited or underdeveloped. Weak cohesion. Limited vocabulary range.
6
Relevant position. Some ideas developed. Coherence is generally maintained. Adequate vocabulary.
7
Clear position throughout. Main ideas extended and supported. Logical paragraphing. Varied vocabulary with some errors.
8
Fully developed argument. Well-supported ideas. Sophisticated cohesion. Wide, precise vocabulary. Rare grammatical errors.

Task 2 Essay Structure (Opinion)

  • Intro (50–70 words): Paraphrase topic → state your position clearly
  • Body 1 (90–110 words): First main argument → explain → example/evidence
  • Body 2 (90–110 words): Second argument or counter-argument + rebuttal
  • Conclusion (40–60 words): Restate position → summarise key points (no new ideas)
  • Aim for exactly 270–290 words to leave time for review

Speaking Practice

11–14 minutes · 3 parts · Examiner-assessed live

Part 1

Introduction & Interview (4–5 min)

Familiar topics — home, family, work, hobbies. Give extended answers (2–3 sentences).

Part 3

Discussion (4–5 min)

Abstract questions linked to Part 2 topic. Show depth of thought.

Part 2

Cue Card (3–4 min)

1 min to prepare, 2 min to speak. Use the cue card bullet points.

Speaking Assessment Criteria (equally weighted)

CriterionWhat examiners look forTips
Fluency & CoherenceSpeaks without long pauses; ideas flow logicallyUse discourse markers: Well, Actually, What I mean is, That said
Lexical ResourceRange and accuracy of vocabulary; avoids repetitionLearn collocations, not just single words
Grammatical RangeUses complex structures; errors don't impede understandingMix tenses; use conditionals, passives, relative clauses
PronunciationClear enough to understand; uses stress and intonationFocus on word stress, not accent

Top Tips

  • Never answer Part 1 questions with just "Yes" or "No" — always add a reason or example
  • In Part 2, structure: describe the topic → explain when/where → say how you felt → why it was memorable
  • In Part 3, use phrases like "From my perspective…", "It's a complex issue, but…", "In contrast to…"
  • Hesitation phrases: "That's an interesting question…", "Let me think about that for a moment…"
  • Record yourself — most learners are surprised by their actual fluency vs. perceived fluency

Listening Practice

30 minutes · 4 sections · 40 questions · Read the transcript and answer questions

Section 1
Social / Daily Life
2 speakers in an everyday conversation — booking, enquiry, registration. 10 questions. Easiest section.
Section 2
Social / Community
1 speaker on a social topic — local facilities, event information, announcements. 10 questions.
Section 3
Educational / Training
2–4 speakers in an academic context — tutorials, project discussions. 10 questions.
Section 4
Academic Lecture
1 speaker delivering a lecture on an academic subject. No pauses. 10 questions. Hardest section.

Question Types

TypeKey Tip
Form completionSpell names/numbers carefully. Answers appear in order.
Multiple choiceRead all options before audio plays. Eliminate distractors.
Sentence completionNo more than 3 words. Check the completed sentence is grammatical.
MatchingAnswers may not follow list order. Listen for paraphrase.
Map / diagram labellingOrientate yourself before audio plays. Use directions as cues.
Short answerStay within the word limit. Use exact words from the recording.

Top Strategies

  • Predict before you listen. Use reading time to predict the answer type (name? number? place?).
  • Listen for paraphrase. Recordings rarely use the exact words of the question — expect synonyms.
  • Write as you listen. Answers in Sections 1–3 appear in order — do not wait until the end.
  • Spell carefully. In form completion, one wrong letter loses the mark.
  • Never leave blanks. There is no penalty for wrong answers — always attempt every question.
  • Beware distractors. Speakers often mention a wrong answer first and then correct it — wait for the final answer.
  • Check grammar. Your answer must fit grammatically into the sentence or form.
  • Transfer carefully. You have 10 minutes at the end — check spelling and that answers are in the right boxes.
